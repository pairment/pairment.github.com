--- layout: docs title: Defining areas of a page to check ---

Developers spend a lot of time developing themes or templates for their site, and it doesn't make sense to give accessibility feedback on things like navigation, headings, or footers to content authors.

Pairment lets you control which areas of a page to check using either custom classes, or the Content Areas section of your site's settings.

Site settings

Under the Content Areas of your site's settings, you can create rules for what pages and sections to check. You can add any valid CSS selector to define the area. For example, if your website wraps user content in:

<div class="content">
My Content!
</div>

Then create a content area with ".content" as the selector, and "/" as the path.
 

Custom classes

Pairment will automatically check any element with a class of "_pairment-content".
