--- layout: docs title: Paragraphs should not replace headings ---
Appropriately used headings are extremely useful for non-sighted users to navigate the structure of the page. Formatting a paragraph as large or bold to look visually like a heading can hide structure from screen readers. For example:   
<p><strong>This is section two</strong></p>
We use paragraph length and contextual clues in the style of all paragraphs of the page to determine if a paragraph should be replaced by a heading.  

Guideline information:

This check applies to WCAG 2.0 Sections:
  • 1.3.1
  • 2.4.10
and WCAG techniques:
  • G141
  • H42
